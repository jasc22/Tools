Goal 1: Continued Education & Training
Goal: Deepen expertise in emerging offensive security domains — specifically AI/LLM security and cloud attack paths — by completing at least two industry-recognized certifications or formal training courses and delivering one internal knowledge-sharing session by the end of the review period.
Description: Pursue training in areas directly aligned with current engagement work, such as AI red teaming, Azure/Entra ID attack techniques, and cloud penetration testing. Complete a minimum of two certifications, courses, or structured training programs (e.g., SANS, OffSec, vendor-specific cloud security tracks) and deliver at least one internal brown-bag or workshop to share key takeaways with the team. Training will be scheduled around engagement cycles, leveraging employer-supported professional development budgets and self-study time. All training completions and the knowledge-sharing session to be accomplished within the 12-month review cycle.

Goal 2: Collaboration
Goal: Strengthen cross-team collaboration by contributing offensive security expertise to internal tooling and detection engineering initiatives throughout the review period.
Description: Partner with internal teams to share knowledge and provide offensive security expertise in support of broader organizational initiatives. Contribute to tooling improvements by offering guidance on cloud attack paths and adversary techniques. Support detection engineering efforts by assisting with the testing and validation of detection capabilities. Actively participate in cross-team exercises and knowledge-sharing sessions to help close the gap between offensive and defensive security operations. Collaboration will be integrated into existing workflows, with a mid-year checkpoint to evaluate progress.
